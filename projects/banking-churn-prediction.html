Case Study 01

Banking Churn
Architecture.

Transforming raw transactional data into a predictive engine. A deep dive into customer attrition through the lens of XGBoost and SHAP explainability.

The Problem

"How do we predict the 'quiet exit' before a customer withdraws their final balance?"

Regional banks face a 20% average annual churn rate. The challenge wasn't just prediction—it was understanding the underlying drivers to empower the retention team with actionable talking points.

The Stack

  • Language Python 3.9
  • Main Library XGBoost
  • Interpretability SHAP
  • Optimization SMOTE

The Methodology.

01. Feature Engineering & Selection

Derived 'Balance-to-Salary' ratios and 'Tenure-Weighted Activity' scores. Cleaned 10k+ records using Scikit-Learn pipelines to prevent data leakage during preprocessing.

02. Handling Class Imbalance

Implemented SMOTE to synthesize minority churn samples. This shifted the model from being "accuracy-biased" to being sensitive to true churn signals.

03. XGBoost Hyper-parameter Tuning

Utilized Random Search CV to optimize tree depth and learning rates, resulting in a 12% lift in F1-score over baseline logistic regression.

Final Reflections.

Key Lesson

Initial accuracy was suspiciously high (99%)—uncovered feature leakage within the 'Estimated Salary' column which was correlated with the synthetic id generation. Correction was vital for real-world validity.

Next Phase

Integrating real-time sentiment analysis from customer support logs to complement transactional features, aiming for a multi-modal predictive model.
